Disney's Ratatouille

Easier than it seems, tatsier than it looks

This Disney ratatouille recipe makes the beautiful dish served up in the movie of the same name. Long and narrow vegetables work best. Serve with crusty bread or over a bed of brown rice, couscous, or pasta.

Ingredients:

  • One can tomato paste
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup minced garlic
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 small eggplant, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 zuchinni, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, trimmed and very thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, coreda and very thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves, or to taste
  • 3 tbsp mascarpone cheese

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 190ÂºC
  2. Spread tomato paste onto the bottom of a 10-inch isquare baking dish. Sprinkle with onion and garlic. Stir in water and 1 tbsp olive oil until thouroughly combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Arrange alterning slices of eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, and yellow bell pepper, starting at the outer edge of the dish and working concentrically towards the center. Overlap slices a little to display colors.
  4. Drizzle vegetables with remaining 3 tbsp olive oil; season with salt and pepper. Sprinkle with thyme leaves. Cover vegetables with a piece of parchment paper cut to fit inside.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until vegetables are roasted and tender, about 45 minutes. Server with dollops of mascarpone cheese.
